### Project Description

The BioCampus MultiPilot (BMP) is a state-of-the-art multi-purpose demonstration facility for industrial biotechnology, located at the Port of Straubing-Sand in Bavaria. As a flagship project funded by the Free State of Bavaria with €80 million, the facility provides unique infrastructure enabling companies, start-ups, and research institutions to develop, test, scale, and optimize their biotechnology processes up to pre-industrial scale.

The facility focuses on the processing of lignocellulosic raw materials and biochemical conversion in reactors with volumes of up to 25 m³. Its flexibly configurable downstream processing technology supports a wide range of production processes — from sustainable plastics and fuels to detergents and specialty chemicals.

---

### Architecture & Construction

The building combines state-of-the-art timber hybrid construction with the stringent demands of industrial architecture. The load-bearing structure integrates timber, steel, and concrete elements, uniting ecological sustainability with high structural performance and architectural quality.

As part of our general planning mandate, we systematically evaluated and compared several ecological facade material alternatives:

- **EcoCocon straw bale panels** — prefabricated timber-frame elements with straw bale cores; Passive House certified, carbon-negative lifecycle
- **Hempcrete (Hanfstein)** — carbon-negative masonry made from hemp and lime, achieving up to –90% CO₂ reduction
- **Solid timber (Vollholz)** — massive timber elements (Holz100), fully circular and ecological
- **Cork brick (Corkbrick)** — circular, natural insulation and facade element
- **Extensive green roof** — ecological multi-use of roof surface (water retention, biodiversity, cooling)

---

### Sustainability

The project is designed in alignment with EU Taxonomy criteria and DGNB standards. The building envelope was systematically evaluated for circularity, ecological material balances, and energy performance. The emphasis on renewable raw materials (timber, straw, hemp, cork) positions the BioCampus MultiPilot as a reference project for sustainable industrial architecture in Bavaria and beyond.
